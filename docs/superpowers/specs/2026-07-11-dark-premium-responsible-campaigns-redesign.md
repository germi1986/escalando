# Dark Premium Landing + Responsible Campaigns

## Objective

Replace the rejected editorial-grid landing with a dark, premium and immediately understandable commercial experience.

The page must communicate three central outcomes:

1. Respond to commercial inquiries with context and human control.
2. Preserve follow-up so opportunities do not depend on memory.
3. Run responsible WhatsApp campaigns when commercial messages have cost, rules and reputation risk.

The result should feel like premium operational software, not a generic AI landing, an editorial magazine, a fintech template or a gaming interface.

## User Feedback Incorporated

The previous direction failed because:

- The light palette did not match the desired brand perception.
- Display typography was too large and competed with the product.
- The visible grid, numbering and dividing rules created visual noise.
- Technical order did not translate into perceptual hierarchy.
- The page required too much effort to identify what mattered first.

The redesign must prioritize clarity before novelty.

## Approved Visual Direction

### Overall Feeling

- Dark premium.
- Calm, modern and trustworthy.
- Product-led, with subtle depth.
- Clear hierarchy visible within a few seconds.
- Technical enough to feel credible, but not cold or industrial.

### Palette

Use a restrained dark palette:

- Primary background: deep graphite, approximately `#090B10`.
- Secondary surface: blue-black graphite, approximately `#11151D`.
- Elevated product surface: approximately `#171C26`.
- Primary text: soft white, approximately `#F4F6FA`.
- Secondary text: cool grey, approximately `#A5ADBA`.
- Commercial accent: electric but controlled blue, approximately `#5B7CFA`.
- Positive operational state: restrained green, approximately `#39D98A`.
- Warning/cost signal: muted amber, approximately `#E7B85C`.

Exact values may be adjusted for contrast. Avoid pure black and saturated neon.

### Typography

- Use a clean contemporary sans-serif for headings and body.
- Technical monospace may appear only in small operational labels and data.
- Hero headline should be large enough to lead, but not dominate the entire viewport.
- Target desktop hero headline: approximately `clamp(2.8rem, 5vw, 5rem)`.
- Body copy should remain comfortably readable at `17–20px` on desktop.
- Avoid all-caps paragraphs, oversized section numbering and compressed editorial tracking.

### Geometry and Depth

- Do not expose a page-wide visual grid.
- Do not use repeated equal-size cards as the primary layout system.
- Use broad sections, natural grouping, spacing and contrast to establish hierarchy.
- Small border radii are allowed for product surfaces and controls; avoid bubbly shapes and excessive pills.
- Use one subtle ambient gradient or glow near the hero/product only.
- Shadows must be soft and restrained.
- No glassmorphism, blur-heavy panels or decorative 3D objects.

## Information Hierarchy

The visitor should understand the page in this order:

1. Escalando Labs helps businesses attend and follow commercial conversations through WhatsApp.
2. AI handles repeatable work while people retain control.
3. The platform also makes paid WhatsApp campaigns safer, more selective and measurable.
4. The system adapts its modules and language to each industry.
5. The visitor can see the product, understand the implementation and evaluate a plan.

Every section must have one dominant idea and one obvious next visual element.

## Page Structure

### 1. Header

- Dark, quiet and compact.
- Brand at left.
- Maximum four navigation items.
- Secondary link to demo.
- One primary CTA: `Evaluar mi operación`.
- Mobile navigation remains accessible and keyboard-friendly.

### 2. Hero

Headline:

`Convertí cada conversación en una oportunidad con próximo paso.`

Supporting copy:

`Escalando Labs reúne WhatsApp, IA y seguimiento comercial para responder mejor, recuperar oportunidades y mantener el control humano cuando realmente importa.`

Actions:

- Primary: `Ver demo`.
- Secondary: `Hablar por WhatsApp`.

The product visual is the hero’s main visual anchor. It should show a calm, believable conversation workspace with:

- active conversation;
- intent or priority;
- AI/human control state;
- suggested next action;
- visible follow-up;
- minimal supporting metrics.

It remains static and server rendered. No Three.js, Canvas, GLB or autonomous motion.

### 3. Outcome Strip

A compact proof line directly after the hero, with three outcomes rather than numbered editorial statements:

- `Respuestas con contexto`;
- `Seguimiento visible`;
- `Campañas con criterio`.

This strip must be visually subordinate to the hero.

### 4. Problem and Solution

Use one broad composition rather than three equal cards.

Left side: the commercial problem.

- Conversations are scattered.
- Follow-up depends on memory.
- Broadcasts or campaigns are sent without enough segmentation or control.

Right side: one integrated operational view showing that the same customer context supports attention, follow-up and campaigns.

### 5. WhatsApp Changed the Rules

This is a new strategic section, positioned after the general problem and before the detailed operating flow.

Headline:

`Cuando cada mensaje cuesta, enviar a todos deja de ser una estrategia.`

Supporting idea:

Businesses previously relied on simple broadcast mechanics. Official commercial messaging now requires more discipline: message categories, approved templates outside the service window, opt-in, opt-out, segmentation, frequency control and protection of number quality.

Do not state that all free broadcast lists were universally removed. Do not publish a permanent fixed price such as USD 65 per 1,000 messages. Pricing varies by market and message category.

The section should visually compare two states without a rigid table:

- Before: manual broadcasts, little measurement, low perceived cost.
- Now: paid delivery, templates, consent, quality risk and need for measurement.

Escalando Labs is presented as the operating layer that helps the business decide:

- who should receive the campaign;
- why they are included;
- what template is used;
- how often they were contacted;
- what the estimated provider cost is;
- what happened after delivery.

Only capabilities already implemented may be described as currently available. Unverified or future capabilities must be framed as implementation scope, roadmap or excluded from the public copy.

### 6. Responsible Campaigns

This is not positioned as bulk messaging or a way to bypass Meta costs.

Core promise:

`No se trata de enviar más. Se trata de enviar con permiso, criterio y una razón comercial.`

The public page may communicate the principles Escalando Labs is designed around:

- documented opt-in;
- clear audience segmentation;
- approved templates;
- contact-frequency control;
- opt-out respect;
- estimated cost visibility;
- campaign objective;
- outcome measurement;
- number-quality protection.

Avoid legal guarantees, guaranteed approval language or claims that Escalando Labs prevents bans.

### 7. How It Works

Use a flowing narrative with four stages, but without a visible grid or large numbering:

- Receive and understand the inquiry.
- Respond using business rules and knowledge.
- Preserve the next commercial action.
- Activate a person or a responsible campaign when appropriate.

A subtle connecting line or progression is acceptable. The hierarchy must come from content and spacing, not boxes.

### 8. Product Depth

Use one larger product showcase with exactly three focused views:

- conversation and takeover;
- customer context and follow-up;
- campaign planning and safeguards.

The three views may overlap slightly or transition through CSS-only interaction, but the default page must remain fully understandable without interaction.

The product visuals must be illustrative and must not imply unavailable production features.

### 9. Industry Adaptability

Explain the configurable-industry model as one concise section:

`Un mismo panel. Módulos, lenguaje y métricas según tu negocio.`

Use a restrained selector or text transition for examples such as gastronomy, services, commerce and appointments. Avoid a 2x2 card matrix.

### 10. Plans

Retain the existing commercial plans and provider-cost clarification unless Germán approves pricing changes separately.

Use three calm vertical plan surfaces with clear hierarchy. They may have modest radius and border contrast, but should not look like generic floating SaaS cards.

The featured plan should be distinguished by accent and content hierarchy, not by excessive scale or glow.

`Campañas responsables` remains part of the Growth plan only if the currently implemented product scope supports the claim.

### 11. FAQ

Keep native accessible disclosure elements.

Add or revise questions covering:

- Meta/provider costs are separate;
- Escalando Labs does not bypass WhatsApp rules;
- campaigns require consent and approved messaging;
- AI does not eliminate human control.

### 12. Final CTA

Use a quiet dark section with one concentrated accent area.

Headline:

`Ordenemos primero lo que hoy te hace perder ventas.`

Primary action:

`Evaluar mi operación`.

Secondary action:

`Ver la demo`.

## Meta and Responsible Messaging Accuracy

Public claims must align with official WhatsApp Business information:

- WhatsApp Business Platform charges per delivered message, with pricing varying by recipient market and message category.
- Marketing messages include promotions, offers, product suggestions and retargeting.
- Businesses may contact users only after receiving the necessary opt-in.
- Opt-out requests must be respected.
- Outside the 24-hour customer service window, business-initiated messages require approved templates.
- Negative feedback and sustained low quality can lead to messaging limits or loss of access.

Reference sources:

- `https://whatsappbusiness.com/products/platform-pricing/`
- `https://whatsappbusiness.com/policy/`

The landing should state principles, not provide legal advice.

## Motion

- Subtle entrance transitions only where they improve comprehension.
- No autoplay scene, rotating hardware or continuous decorative motion.
- Prefer opacity and short vertical translation.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Product status indicators may pulse very gently, but must not distract.

## Accessibility and Responsive Behavior

- Maintain WCAG-friendly contrast.
- Preserve visible keyboard focus.
- Use semantic heading order.
- Minimum interactive target size approximately 44px.
- Mobile hero becomes one column with copy before product.
- Product visual simplifies by hiding nonessential side panels, not by shrinking text excessively.
- No horizontal overflow.
- Native details remain for FAQ and may remain for mobile navigation.

## Technical Scope

Modify only:

- public home component;
- landing-specific CSS;
- consent-banner landing overrides if required;
- landing design and implementation documentation.

Do not modify:

- Supabase;
- authentication;
- RLS;
- panel routes or components;
- APIs;
- n8n;
- WhatsApp webhooks or sending logic;
- campaign backend logic;
- analytics;
- environment variables;
- legal pages;
- pricing values without separate approval.

Keep the prior landing implementations available for rollback until the new visual direction is accepted.

## Validation

- Run the production build successfully.
- Confirm TypeScript completes without errors.
- Confirm `/` returns HTTP 200 on the branch preview.
- Confirm `/demo`, `/contacto`, `/privacidad`, `/terminos`, `/eliminacion-datos` and `/api/meta/conversions` still build.
- Confirm no active Three.js, Canvas or GLB dependency is imported by the home route.
- Review desktop and mobile visually.
- Confirm the page contains no universal claim that Meta removed all free broadcast lists.
- Confirm the page does not use a permanent USD-per-1,000-message claim.
- Confirm all campaign capability claims match the current product.

## Success Criteria

A first-time visitor should be able to answer within approximately ten seconds:

- What does Escalando Labs do?
- Why is it useful now?
- How does it help with WhatsApp campaigns responsibly?
- What should I click next?

The page should feel premium and ordered without showing the layout system that creates that order.
